GN Story Walkthrough

The collection of screenshots below illustrate a user story that could be helpful for improving the GolfNow site.

As a relatively new golfer, I often avoid booking tee times where I might be paired with strangers, preferring to play only with my spouse. This behavior can lead to lost revenue for the golf course, as we only book slots with all four spaces available. This not only reduces the course's potential income from additional players but also affects customer satisfaction. By improving the pairing system, we can better match golfers of similar skill levels, enhancing the overall experience and encouraging more frequent bookings.

The following screenshots illustrate a potential solution to this problem. The level of golfer could be selected at checkout time, as part of the player's profile, or from other play metrics that may be available with the user's account, such as completed scorecards and course feedback.

Disclaimer: I do not work for or represent GolfNow; I am just a frequent customer and fellow technologist. Thanks for viewing!

In this screenshot, the customer is presented with the levels of play for partially-filled tee times, allowing them to select a tee time and play level that matches their preferred grouping for the day.

When selecting a tee time that has been previously unselected, they are prompted for the level of play that would be most compatible with their group, in order to let other potential players know the expectation for their pair.

This screenshot shows an alternative presentation based on customer profile information. Tee times that match the customer's preferences or profile are highlighted in the grid or list view. It contains a helpful tooltip to explain the highlight.
